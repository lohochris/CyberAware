Understanding Online Abuse and Harassment

Online abuse can take many forms and may affect adults in different ways. This section explains common types of online abuse, how they may affect individuals, and why it is important to recognise them early.

What Is Online Abuse?

Online abuse refers to harmful or threatening behaviour carried out through digital platforms such as social media, email, or messaging services. It may be repeated or occur as a single serious incident.

The following video provides a general overview of online abuse and is included for educational purposes only.

Common Types of Online Abuse

  • Repeated insulting or abusive messages
  • Threatening or intimidating behaviour
  • Harassment in public comment sections
  • Sharing personal information without consent
  • Persistent unwanted contact (online stalking)
  • Non-consensual sharing or threats to share images

How Online Abuse Can Affect Adults

Experiences of online abuse may affect emotional wellbeing, sleep, work performance, relationships, and confidence in participating online. The impact varies from person to person and should not be minimised.

Why Awareness Matters

Understanding what constitutes online abuse helps individuals recognise harmful behaviour, take appropriate action, and seek support when needed.
