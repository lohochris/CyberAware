What You Can Do

If you are experiencing online abuse or harassment, there are practical steps you can take to protect yourself and seek support. The guidance below is for educational purposes and may help you decide what to do next.

Step 1: Preserve Evidence

Keep records of abusive messages or posts. This may include screenshots, message URLs, dates, times, and usernames. Avoid altering the evidence.

Step 2: Use Platform Tools

Most social media and messaging platforms provide tools to block, mute, or report abusive behaviour. Reporting within the platform can help stop further contact.

Step 3: Consider Reporting Externally

If the behaviour is threatening, persistent, or involves sharing personal images without consent, you may consider contacting the police or a relevant organisation for advice.

Step 4: Seek Support

Online abuse can affect wellbeing. Speaking to a trusted person or contacting a support service may help you feel less isolated.
